        "\n# Micro time gated correlation\n\nThe background in the fluorescence intensity traces affects the computed\nfluorescence correlation functions. In single-molecule spectroscopy the\nbackground is mainly caused by scattered light. Thus, by discriminating\nphotons with small micro times a large fraction of the background can be eliminated.\n\nNote, the example that used the micro times of the detected photons to gate\naway the scattered light fraction in a single-molecule experiment can be easily\nmodified to correlate the prompt and the delayed excitation in a pulsed\ninterleaved excitation experiment.\n"
