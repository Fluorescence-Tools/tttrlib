Using the combined\ntime axis of a so called full correlation can be performed in continuous wave excitation\nexperiments in contrast to the usually performed pulsed excitation.\n\nThe example below illustrates how a full correlation can be computed. Note, in\nthe example the full correlation is computed for a sample that was measured in a\npulsed excitation experiment. However, the same procedure can be applied to cw data.\n\nThis example illustrates a normal correlation and demonstrates two approaches\nhow to compute full correlations with ``tttrlib``.\n"
