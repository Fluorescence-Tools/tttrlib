        "\n# Estimating the noise of the correlation curves\n\n## Introduction\n\nThere are many approaches to estimate the noise in correlation functions\n(see: :cite:`Wohland2001`, :cite:`Qian1990`, :cite:`Starchev2001`). An\nanalytical calculation of the noise is difficult because it involves diverging\nintegrals for the correlations decaying functions :cite:`Wohland2001`. For TTTR\ndata an analytical solution of the noise in the correlation is however not\nnecessary, as the data contained in the photon stream can be split to yield a set\nof correlation functions that is used to yield an estimate for the expected\ncorrelation function and the associated noise by the mean and the standard\ndeviation :cite:`Kapusta2007`, :cite:`Enderlein1997`, :cite:`Bohmer2002`.\n\nHere it is illustrated how the data is split into multiple subsets to estimate the\nnoise for quantitative analysis of correlation curves.\n"
