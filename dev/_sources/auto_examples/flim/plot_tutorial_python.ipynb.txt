In the example, there are overall 41 frames are present\nin the file each having 256 lines. As the last frame is often incomplete (see Figure\nabove) the last frame is neglected (41 - 1 = 40). Here, there are two options: 1)\neither the total time from the beginning of each new line (line start) to the beginning\nof the next line is considered as a line or 2) the time between the line start and\nthe line stop is considered as the time base to calculate the pixel duration. In\nthe first case, the back movement of the laser to the line start can be visualized\nin the image. In the later case, only the *valid* region where the laser scans over\nthe sample is visualized. For most applications the later approach is useful. To\nunderstand the microscope laser scanner the former approach is more useful. Above,\n``line_duration_valid`` is the time the laser spends in every of the lines in a\nvalid region and ``line_duration_total`` is the total time the laser spends in a\nline including the rewind to the line beginning.         "Before creating an image the channel numbers of the frame and the line markers need\nto be determined.
